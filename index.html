Apollo 11: First Moon Landing

Apollo 11 launched on July 16, 1969, from Kennedy Space Center in Florida atop the powerful Saturn V rocket. The crew consisted of three astronauts: Neil Armstrong (Commander), Buzz Aldrin (Lunar Module Pilot), and Michael Collins (Command Module Pilot). The mission lasted 8 days, 3 hours, and 18 minutes, ending with a safe splashdown in the Pacific Ocean on July 24.
